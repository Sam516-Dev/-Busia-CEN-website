About Us

Learn more about Busia County Education Network (Busia CEN) and our mission to fight misinformation and promote accountability.

Background

Busia County faces growing challenges of misinformation that affect youth accountability, women in leadership, and civic participation. Under the DWA TrustLab Incubator project (2025), Busia CEN is establishing a community-driven fact-checking platform to strengthen public trust and informed participation in governance.

Our Mission

To empower communities in Busia to identify, challenge, and counter misinformation by promoting digital literacy, transparency, and civic engagement through verified information and dialogue.

Our Objectives

  • Establish a responsive fact-checking and community engagement platform.
  • Promote participatory reporting and user-driven claim submissions.
  • Build Busia CEN’s editorial and technical capacity for sustainability.
  • Connect with regional networks like Code for Africa’s AFCA.

Expected Results

  • A fully functional community fact-checking website for Busia.
  • Dozens of verified fact-checks and community reports each year.
  • Trained local ambassadors and editorial staff producing credible content.
  • Greater awareness and resilience against misinformation across the county.

Support & Collaboration

Busia CEN welcomes partnerships for technical support, mentorship, and amplification of verified content. Together, we can build a more informed and accountable community.
